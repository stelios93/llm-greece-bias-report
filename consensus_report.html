Methodology: Position Strength Framework

Why "Neutral" Is Not Neutral

The standard 1-5 scoring scale treats 3 (Neutral) as the baseline. But for questions with established factual answers, a "neutral" response is not neutral at all — it manufactures false equivalence.

Example: "Was Alexander the Great Greek?" — Scholarly consensus is overwhelmingly yes. A model responding "it's debatable" or "both sides have a point" is not being balanced; it is manufacturing ambiguity on a settled question.
